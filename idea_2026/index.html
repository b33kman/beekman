Prompt Guidance

Build every prompt on the R-C-T-F-C framework. The more parts you provide, the closer the first response lands — the starter prompt below is this framework, filled in.

The R-C-T-F-C framework
R
Role
Who you want the AI to be.
C
Context
The background it needs to work.
T
Task
The specific thing to do, stated plainly.
F
Format
How to structure the answer.
C
Constraints
The boundaries: length, tone, sources.
The starter prompt — R-C-T-F-C, filled in
Copy & run on one de-identified document
"You are an experienced legal assistant supporting a special education hearing officer. I will paste one de-identified document from a due process matter. Summarize the document, identify the issues it presents, and list every date or deadline it references. Format your answer with bold headings: Summary, Issues Presented, Dates & Deadlines, Open Questions. Use only the document provided; do not cite case law or outside sources; do not include PII, replace with generic terms. If something is not stated in the document, write 'not stated.' Keep it under 750 words."

Advanced — automate it with a routine

Don't chase the news — have it delivered. Paste this into ChatGPT, Claude, or Copilot as a scheduled task (a Session 2 lesson in practice).

The Weekly Routine
"Every Monday at 8:00 a.m., search for major AI product and model updates from OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, and Microsoft over the past week, plus any significant news about AI use in the legal profession. Summarize in five bullets or fewer, in plain language, with a link for each item. Skip rumors and speculation; include only announced products and published reports."
